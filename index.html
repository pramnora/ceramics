Ceramics, Level 1

Introduction

Hi, my name is, Paul Ramnora. I'm currently studying at WMCollege, Mornington Crescent branch.

Course(12 x weeks): Ceramics - Level I, with teacher: Tana West.

I have tried doing introductory Ceramics before...

  • Lambeth College: Adare Centre
  • Morley College(arts based)

...; but, then, the emphasis was purely on 'creating'/and, involved no note taking/homework.

This is my first introduction to learning how to do Ceramics, seriously; meaning, I have to...

  • Create work
  • Take careful notes/photos about each creation step
  • Keep a sketchbook...; and, use it to plan work.
  • Complete doing homework tests online
  • Also, show studio health and safety

As somebody who suffers from ADD/Attention Deficit Disorder...; therefore, my mind tends to wonder off, constantly, going all over the place...; and, thus, I find it really difficult indeed to maintain any 'strict' focus.

I really do enjoy being on the course. I attend/participate in each lesson. I also watch others to learn how they do things, too(use the potters wheel/-etc.). I take some notes...; plus, regularly, do 'ceramics related' online research both during lunch breaks/as well as, after class/-etc.

However, ADD/Attention Deficit Disorder...will often lead to such things as forgetfulness/and, poor organisation...

  • In the absolute rush to both get ready, and, leave home...in order to arrive at class on time...I totally forgot to bring my iPad/iPhone along with me...; so, I couldn't record nothing...?
  • My note taking hasn't been as properly extensive as it should be...so,if there were 6 steps to be recorded; maybe, I only recorded 3...?!
  • As to getting homework in on time...; well, frankly, I didn't!
  • I did buy some sculpting tools to use from off eBay...; but, then, again, I didn't always remember to bring those tools in...???
  • Instead, of doing the homework, properly, the teachers way...such as show a Powerpoint presentation; instead, I went and did it my 'own' way, instead, by creating a series of web pages. (I felt within these web pages I could 'say/show/share' far more.)

Nevertheless, even if I don't pass the course exams...; being on this course, I've managed to learn far more about Ceramic creation processes than ever I knew before; and, for this I will always remain, well and truly, grateful.

In conclusion, my interest in learning more about the rich art of ceramics creation..had begun long before this course ever started...at around age 16/and, now, I'm aged 60. I always loved to see: Greek/Roman/Chinese pottery...as well as, modern day pottery...; in particular, the works of Pablo Picasso. -(I used to be a regular plate collector on the Bradford exchange...collecting plates by Norman Rockwell/-etc. I also like to collect ceramic sculptures from 2nd hand shops.)- So, once this course has ended, I'm sure, my interest in both creating and learning more about Ceramic creation processes will still be ongoing...because, if anything...then, being a part of the course has increased my enthusiasm for studying learning how to do ceramics all the more/as opposed to having in any way at all dampened it.

If there is any one lesson I learned in particular it's that creating ceramic 'masterpieces' is nowhere near as easy as it looks...?! (I used to visit Harrords Knightsbridge store/and, also, visit Bond Street shops where I admired the works of Lallique/Wedgewood/-etc.)? I wanted to create a masterpiece head/or, dancer...? But, all the time, I was very deeply 'aware' that I lacked the necessary 'fine art' related skills. Namely, in order to be successful at producing ''human forms'; then, you have to know 'anatomy' really well. In order to create a hand with fingers...; it takes much effort/persevation/concentration/knowledge/patience...; I didn't really have those skills.../or, maybe, I just needed more time to be able to develop it...? The thing about studying art -(or, maybe, any other subject which you are truly passionate about)- is...you must never ever quit...; then, who knows, maybe, one day...either sooner or later...you will achieve what are your own end goals...???
